# Studio Cucurbits Design System

## Direction

An editorial music-and-creative-technology studio website. The page must explain services quickly while retaining a sense of experimentation. Use a white or faintly warm white field, black typography, precise hairlines, and a single vermilion-orange signal color only for active states or a key action.

## Typography

- UI and body: Helvetica, Arial, Japanese system sans.
- Display: the same sans family, large and plain; no condensed, ornamental, or futuristic typeface.
- Japanese and English may sit together, but never overlap.
- Dot-matrix wordmarks use circular black dots, not a pixel font.

## Visual Grammar

- Large areas of white space.
- 1px black or soft-gray dividers.
- Square or subtly rounded controls only.
- Dots behave like sonic particles: gather, pause, disperse, and form a wordmark.
- Avoid gradients, glass effects, cards-inside-cards, and decorative imagery.

## Home Page Content Order

1. Dot-animation logo reveal.
2. Studio positioning: `Music / Sound / AI / Creative Technology`.
3. Clear service index.
4. AI position: expansion versus human judgment.
5. Three-step process.
6. People: Sachie Kobayashi and Frederik Bous.
7. Selected work and inquiry.

## Responsive Rules

- Desktop: spacious two-column or 12-column editorial grid.
- Mobile: single column; dot animation remains visible but becomes shorter.
- Navigation may collapse to a simple compact row; no horizontal text rotation.
